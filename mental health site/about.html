About Student Mental Health Support

Our mission, values, and commitment to student wellbeing

Our Mission

Empowering students to thrive through mental health support and education

Founded in 2015, Student Mental Health Support was created to address the growing mental health needs of college and university students. We recognize that academic success is deeply connected to emotional wellbeing, and we're committed to providing accessible, evidence-based resources to support the whole student.

Our programs are designed by mental health professionals in collaboration with students, ensuring our approaches are both clinically sound and genuinely helpful for the unique challenges students face today.

50,000+ Students served annually
200+ Campus partners
95% Of students report reduced stress

Our Values

The principles that guide our work

Compassion

We meet students where they are with empathy and without judgment, recognizing that each person's journey is unique.

Evidence-Based

Our resources and programs are grounded in scientific research and best practices in mental health care.

Inclusivity

We actively work to create services that are accessible and relevant to students of all backgrounds and identities.

Confidentiality

Student privacy is paramount. We maintain strict confidentiality standards in all our services.

Peer Support

We believe in the power of students helping students through shared experiences.

Prevention

We focus on building resilience and coping skills before crises occur.

Our Approach

How we support student mental health

1. Prevention & Education

We provide workshops, online resources, and awareness campaigns to help students build resilience and recognize when they might need support.

2. Early Intervention

Through screening tools and peer support programs, we help identify challenges early when they're most manageable.

3. Accessible Support

We offer multiple pathways to assistance including self-help tools, peer counseling, and professional services.

4. Campus Collaboration

We work with faculty, staff, and student organizations to create a campus culture that supports mental wellbeing.
