Stress Management

Techniques to help manage academic and personal stress

The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

When feeling overwhelmed, pause and notice: 5 things you can see, 4 things you can touch, 3 things you can hear, 2 things you can smell, and 1 thing you can taste. This brings you back to the present moment.

2 min exercise Easy

Journaling

Spend 10 minutes writing about your thoughts and feelings. This can help process emotions and reduce stress.

Movement Breaks

Take a 5-minute walk every hour. Physical activity boosts endorphins and clears your mind.

Music Therapy

Create playlists for different moods - energizing, calming, or focusing. Music can significantly impact your emotional state.

Sleep Wellness

Improving sleep quality for better mental health

The 90-Minute Sleep Cycle

Plan sleep in 90-minute increments (1.5hr, 3hr, 4.5hr, 6hr, 7.5hr, 9hr) to wake between sleep cycles. This helps reduce grogginess and improve alertness.

Requires planning Moderate Try Sleep Calculator

Digital Sunset

Avoid screens 1 hour before bed. The blue light disrupts melatonin production, making it harder to fall asleep.

Evening Nutrition

Avoid heavy meals, caffeine, and alcohol 3 hours before bedtime. These can disrupt sleep quality.

Wind Down Routine

Develop a consistent 30-minute pre-sleep routine to signal your brain it's time to rest.

Mindfulness Practices

Cultivating present-moment awareness

Breath Awareness Meditation

Practice focusing on your breath for 5-10 minutes daily. When your mind wanders, gently return attention to your breathing. This builds focus and reduces anxiety.

5-10 min Beginner

Mindful Eating

Eat without distractions, savoring each bite. Notice textures, flavors, and how the food makes you feel.

Guide

Nature Observation

Spend 5 minutes observing nature - clouds, trees, or birds. This grounds you in the present moment.

Gratitude Journal

Write down 3 things you're grateful for each day. This shifts focus to positive aspects of life.

Social Connection

Building and maintaining healthy relationships

The 5:1 Ratio

Maintain a ratio of 5 positive interactions for every 1 challenging one in relationships. This creates healthy social bonds.

Ongoing Moderate

Study Buddies

Combine socializing with academics by forming study groups. This reduces isolation and improves learning.

Active Listening

Practice fully focusing on the speaker without planning your response. This deepens connections.

Social Scheduling

Treat social time as important appointments. Schedule regular check-ins with friends and family.

Academic Balance

Managing coursework while maintaining wellbeing

The Pomodoro Technique

Work for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After 4 cycles, take a 15-30 minute break. This maintains focus while preventing burnout.

25 min sessions Moderate
Start Timer

Task Prioritization

Use the Eisenhower Matrix to categorize tasks by urgency and importance. Focus on what truly matters.

Time Blocking

Assign specific times for different activities, including relaxation. This creates structure while ensuring balance.

Say No Strategically

Practice setting boundaries by declining non-essential commitments that would overload your schedule.
